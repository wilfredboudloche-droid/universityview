What is Academic Integrity?

Honesty

Submitting only your own work and giving credit where credit is due.

Trust

Building a foundation of reliability between students and educators.

Fairness

Ensuring equal opportunity for all students to demonstrate their learning.

Responsibility

Taking ownership of your academic journey and its outcomes.

Types of Academic Misconduct

Plagiarism
Using someone else's words, ideas, or work without proper attribution. This includes copying from websites, books, or other students' work.
Cheating
Using unauthorized materials, information, or assistance during an exam or assignment. This includes using notes when not allowed or getting answers from others.
Fabrication
Inventing or falsifying data, citations, or information in academic work. This includes making up sources or altering research results.
Collusion
Unauthorized collaboration on assignments meant to be completed individually. Working together when independent work is required.

When to Cite

Direct quotes, paraphrased ideas, statistics, images, and any information that isn't common knowledge.

Citation Styles

MLA, APA, Chicago, and other formats have specific rules. Always follow your instructor's requirements.
