Case 1 – “It crashes… but only later.” (Overheating & instability)

Case 1 Takeaways (what a technician remembers)

Case 2 – “Power light is on… but the screen is blank.” (No image)

A user reports no image on a monitor/projector. Your goal is to troubleshoot power, the input source (OSD), and cabling using the known good technique.

Lesson 4C reminder:
  • First ensure the display is plugged in, on, and not in standby.
  • Use the monitor’s OSD to select the correct input source (HDMI vs DVI, etc.).
  • Check cables for loose/stretch/crimp and confirm the cable matches the use case (ex: 4K may require a High‑Speed HDMI cable).
  • To rule out cable problems, substitute a known good cable or test the display with a different PC.

Case 3 – “The picture looks wrong.” (Artifacts, color, flicker, and projector shutdowns)

Use Lesson 4C’s video-quality and projector troubleshooting guidance to identify the most likely cause and best corrective step.

Key symptom → fix cues from Lesson 4C:
  • Fuzzy image: often a resolution mismatch with native resolution; adjust resolution or update driver.
  • Flashing/flicker: check/secure cable; could be failing backlight circuitry; a faulty/overheating video card can also cause flashing.
  • Burn-in: prevent via screen saver or auto-off during inactivity (OLED/plasma more prone).
  • Color glitches (purple/green lines): often loose/faulty/low-quality connector/cable → try replacing the cable.
  • Projector shutdown: typically overheating → check fan/vents/ambient temp; if not, check loose cables, bulb seating, firmware updates.

Case 3 Takeaways
  • Fuzzy/blurry often = resolution mismatch → set to native resolution / update driver.
  • Flicker/flashing = check cabling first; also consider video card overheating; isolate by testing with another PC.
  • Color glitches often = loose/faulty/low-quality cabling → replace cable.
  • Projector shutdowns typically = overheating → fan/vents/ambient temperature checks first.
